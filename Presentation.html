Problem Statement

Manual SEC filing analysis is slow and hard to verify

Pain Point

10-K and 10-Q filings are long, dense, and time-consuming to read manually.

Risk

Manual summaries can miss details and usually do not preserve page-level traceability.

Goal

Support natural-language questions over filings with grounded answers and inline page citations.

Approach

Use a RAG pipeline combining semantic retrieval, metadata filters, and controlled LLM prompting.

Chunking & Retrieval

Chunking is designed to preserve financial meaning

  • Paragraph-first extraction from the PDF.
  • Adjacent related paragraphs merged into semantic chunks.
  • Each chunk stores page-span metadata.
  • Preserves multi-paragraph financial discussion.
  • Helps avoid broken context around metrics and disclosures.
  • Enables traceable inline citations in final answers.

Dataset Setup

Golden datasets, collection method, and usage

What the datasets contain
  • Two separate 25-question JSONL sets.
  • Pratheek set: IDs 001 to 025.
  • Aditya set: IDs 021 to 045.
  • Each row has question, expected answer or facts, source pages, and retrieval keywords.
How data was collected
  • Questions were generated with LLM assistance using GPT 5.4 and Sonnet 4.6.
  • The generated outputs were structured into JSONL benchmark rows.
  • Expected facts and source pages were then validated against the Microsoft 2025 10-K text.
  • Retrieval keywords were added for relevance checks, and two files were maintained for ownership split.
